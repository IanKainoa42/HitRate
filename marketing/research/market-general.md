# HitRate — General Market Research: Trick / Skill Outcome Tracking

*Prepared 2026-06-06. Positioning under review: pivot from "cheer stunt counter" to general-purpose attempt-level outcome tracker (4-level Landed / Sketchy / Bail / Slam ladder, hit-rate dashboard, holographic share cards, offline, no accounts).*

## 2. The key question — does anyone own "attempt-level success-rate across any skill"?

**No. The space is fragmented per-vertical, and even within verticals the outcome-rate concept is rarely the product.**

- **Cross-skill attempt-rate is unclaimed.** The only sport-agnostic candidates are habit RPGs (binary +/− habit ratio) and dumb tally counters (no semantics). Neither frames the user as "an athlete grinding a trick to consistency."
- **Per-vertical winners are narrow and taxonomy-locked.** KAYA owns climbing send/project logging — welded to grades; will never generalize. HomeCourt/ShotTracker own basketball make/miss — binary, single-sport, sensor-first.
- **The graded outcome ladder is the genuinely white space.** Nobody offers a *renamable 4-level severity ladder* as the core primitive. Existing apps are binary or state-based. The ladder captures the "I landed it but it was ugly" reality skaters, climbers, and gymnasts all live in.
- **HitRate's defensible combination = graded ladder × manual one-tap × any-skill × offline/no-account × holographic share card.** Each ingredient exists somewhere; the *combination* exists nowhere. The spectator/parent-logging mode is also under-served outside heavy coach platforms.

**Skeptical caveat:** "white space" partly means "no one has proven demand for it generically." Users self-identify by sport, not by "I track outcomes." HitRate's risk is not a competitor — it's that generic positioning is *harder to market* than any single vertical (see §4).

## 3. Keyword landscape (general positioning)

| Keyword | Winnable? | Read |
|---|---|---|
| **"trick tracker"** | **Yes — winnable.** | Low competition, on-intent. Best primary term. |
| **"landed tracker" / "landed counter"** | **Yes — wide open.** | Almost no apps target it. Reads natively to skate/snow/BMX. |
| **"skate counter" / "skate tracker"** | **Contested but small.** | Wheels/Ollee/Sk8te compete on GPS/hardware, not outcomes. Secondary vertical term. |
| **"skill tracker"** | **Risky — diluted.** | Collides with habit apps, HR SaaS, "10,000 hours" apps. Don't anchor here. |
| **"progress tracker tricks" / "trick progress"** | **Yes — winnable long-tail.** | Low competition, high intent. |
| **"consistency tracker"** | **Dominated — avoid as primary.** | Habit-tracker-giant turf. Benefit copy only, not a keyword bet. |
| **"hit rate" / "success rate" tracker** | **Mixed.** | Collides with baseball/sales/poker meanings (§5). Weak acquisition keyword. |

**Net keyword strategy:** anchor on **"trick tracker" + "landed tracker" + "[sport] trick tracker"** long-tails. Avoid "skill tracker" and "consistency tracker" as primaries.

## 4. Vertical-by-vertical wedge ranking

Scored on (a) audience size, (b) sharing culture, (c) absence of an outcome incumbent.

| Rank | Vertical | Audience | Share culture | Incumbent gap | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Skateboarding** | Large, young, mobile-native | **Highest** — IG/TikTok clip culture; "landed it" clips are the native content unit | **Wide open** — incumbents are GPS, hardware, or tutorials | **Best wedge.** The holo share card maps perfectly onto clip culture. |
| **2** | **Cheer (home turf)** | Mid, but Ian's existing reach/credibility | Moderate-high | Already proven fit | **Keep as launch beachhead.** Lowest CAC; validated core while skate is the growth bet. |
| **3** | **Climbing** | Large, fast-growing, affluent | High, but channeled through KAYA/Mountain Project | **Occupied** — KAYA owns the logbook slot | **Hard.** Enter only with a "graded-attempt-rate" angle KAYA can't match. |
| **4** | **Gymnastics / tumbling** | Mid, structured | Moderate (parent-driven) | Chalk owns *meet scores* + ships share cards; practice-attempt outcomes open | **Adjacent to cheer; viable but coach-gated.** Strong spectator/parent fit. |
| **5** | **BMX / parkour / freestyle soccer** | Small-to-mid each | High but fragmented | Wide open (tutorial apps only) | **Long-tail expansion, not a launch wedge.** Absorb later once skate proves it. |

**Recommendation:** Launch **cheer-validated → skate-led**. Climbing is a trap (loved incumbent). Gymnastics is a credible adjacent. BMX/parkour/soccer ride along free once the generic ladder works.

## 5. Naming check — "HitRate"

**Inside the app stores: clear.** No consumer iOS app named "HitRate." Collisions found:
- **Hitrate.app** — B2B retail camera-analytics SaaS. Different category; owns the `.app` domain (acquisition would be contested).
- **Hit Factor Calculator** — competitive-shooting scoring; unrelated.
- **HypeRate** — Twitch heart-rate streaming; phonetically near — minor verbal-confusion risk.

**Vernacular — the honest concern.** "Hit rate" does **not** read cleanly as "landed tricks" outside cheer:
- **Baseball:** "hit rate" = batting average. Dominant association.
- **Sales/marketing/poker:** conversion / win frequency.
- **Skate vernacular:** native words are **"landed" / "stomped" / "made it."** Skaters don't say "my hit rate on kickflips."
- **Climbing vernacular:** **"send," "flash," "project."** "Hit" is foreign.

**Verdict:** safe legally/store-wise but semantically off-target for the general pivot. Cheer *does* say "we hit the routine" — a cheer-specific idiom. Mitigations: (a) keep "HitRate" as the brand, make the *subtitle/tagline* carry the meaning ("Track every attempt. Any trick."); or (b) consider a name built on the native verb — anything around **"Landed"** is both an open keyword (§3) and cross-vernacular for board/air sports. Don't assume the name travels; it's the weakest part of the pivot.
