# HitRate — Market Research (Cheer vertical)

**App concept:** Tap-once-per-rep stunt-outcome counter for cheer. Outcome taxonomy (hit / bobble / building fall / major fall) → hit-rate dashboard with trends + leaderboards → shareable holographic "Stunt Cards" to Instagram. Free/cheap, offline, no accounts. Audience: all-star and school cheer athletes + coaches, US.

**Bottom line up front:** The cheer-specific stunt-tracking niche is nearly empty on iOS. The single genuinely overlapping app (Cheer Trainer) has ~10 ratings and is positioned as coach/team-management software with a subscription, and its tracking is binary hit/miss — not an outcome-severity ladder. The "athlete-first, tap-fast, shareable card" model is proven viable by a gymnastics app (Chalk, $9.99/yr, generates shareable score cards) but **does not exist for cheer**. The opportunity is real but the demand is quiet: there is no visible community clamor for this, so HitRate likely has to create the behavior, not capture existing search traffic.

## 1. Competitor scan

Scoped to apps that actually overlap HitRate's job-to-be-done (track stunt/skill outcomes, count reps, or share athlete stats).

| App | Price / model | One-line positioning | Review themes (good / bad) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cheer Trainer** (id6470613193) | Free + sub ($3.99/mo, $35.99/yr) | The only true direct overlap: coach-run cheer training platform with **live hit/miss tracking, real-time analytics, leaderboards**, skill check-offs, S&C plans, attendance. | **Good:** coaches praise time saved on hit/miss math, "data-driven," intuitive. **Bad:** signup bugs (dev says fixed). **Signal:** only ~4.0 stars on **10 ratings** — near-zero traction despite owning the core feature. |
| **Chalk: Gymnastics Tracker** (id6757092299) | Free + "Chalk Pro" $9.99/yr | Athlete-first gymnastics meet-score tracker; clean, single-purpose; **"Chalkboard" generates shareable image stat cards** for IG/TikTok. Closest *model* analog to HitRate (just another sport). | **Good:** "user friendly and affordable," fast on meet day, loves no monthly sub, loves social sharing + "Road to 40" calc. **Bad:** none surfaced (5.0 on **5 ratings** — brand new). |
| **ScoreCat** (id6667094618) | Free + sub ($3.99/mo, $39.99/yr) | Gymnastics live-meet score tracking + per-athlete stats, videos, follow/notify. | **Good:** real-time results, clean UI, few ads. **Bad:** "same info is free elsewhere" — recurring pushback on the subscription; wants better filtering. (4.0 on **90 ratings** — most-traction comp here.) |
| **Skilltrakker** (id1560044502) | Free + sub $8.99/mo | Gymnastics daily-drills + streak/points gamification. | **Good:** kids "improve so much," no forced login on daily challenge. **Bad:** "**ZERO functionality without paying $9/month**," level-switch bugs. (2.6 on **28 ratings** — cautionary tale on hard paywall.) |
| **Cheerleading Skills / Cheer RX** (id690307830 / id881568795) | Instructional content | "How to do stunts/tosses/jumps" tutorial-content apps. Adjacent (cheer skills) but **teaching, not tracking** — no counter, no stats. | Listed for completeness; low relevance. |
| **Tally counter apps** (Tally, Counter Tally Count, etc.) | Free / one-time ~$1–5 | Generic single-tap counters; the actual fallback cheer people use to "count" anything. | **Good:** fast, simple, cheap. **Bad:** zero domain meaning — no outcome taxonomy, no hit-rate %, nothing shareable. These are HitRate's real incumbent "competitor" by default behavior. |

**What is NOT a competitor:**
- **CHEER Official 3D** (id1581111883) — a 3D choreography **video game**. Competes for entertainment time, not HitRate's job.
- **CheerCounts** (cheercounts.com) — formation/choreo + team-management web/Android platform; iOS listed "coming soon." Overlaps FormationFlow more than HitRate.
- **Stunt.fit** — all-in-one cheer team SaaS (web). No iOS App Store listing found.
- **Studio Pro / iClassPro / Skillshark / TeamSnap / Cheerletics** — gym-CRM / evaluation software sold to gym owners. Different buyer, different price tier.

## 2. Keyword landscape

| Term | Who owns it now | Winnable for a new app? |
|---|---|---|
| **"cheer counter" / "stunt counter"** | Nobody. Returns generic *tally* apps. | **Yes — wide open.** Strongest play. |
| **"stunt tracker"** | Lightly contested (Cheer Trainer marketing, gym SaaS — mostly web). | **Yes.** No dominant iOS title. |
| **"cheer stats" / "cheerleading stats"** | CheerStats.us (competition scores, web) — not athlete rep-stats apps. | **Yes for the app store.** |
| **"hit rate" / "stunt hit rate"** | Unowned as an app concept; coach jargon, not a search term yet. | **Winnable but low volume** — positioning phrase, not a discovery channel. |
| **"cheer trainer" / "cheer training"** | Cheer Trainer app + many S&C apps. | **Avoid as primary** — crowded and off-position. |
| **"cheer app"** (head term) | CHEER Official 3D (game), SaaS, FormationFlow. | **Dominated** — rank for long-tail instead. |

**Read:** Anchor ASO on **"cheer counter," "stunt tracker," "cheer stats."** Caveat: low keyword volume means social discovery (TikTok/IG demo of the tap-and-share card) will matter more than App Store search alone.

## 3. Pricing norms

- **Free + cheap annual ("affordable, no monthly"):** Chalk — $9.99/year — *praised in reviews* for avoiding monthly subs. Sweet spot for athlete/parent-paid single-purpose tools.
- **Free + subscription ($3.99–$8.99/mo):** Cheer Trainer, ScoreCat, Skilltrakker. Consistent review backlash. Monthly subs work only with an ongoing data/service feed — **not** a self-contained counter.
- **One-time / free generic utilities:** tally counters are free or ~$1–5 one-time. That's the price *anchor* for "a thing that counts taps."

**Recommendation:** Free with a one-time unlock or low annual (≤$9.99/yr) for premium (extra holo card themes, advanced trends, multi-athlete leaderboards). Avoid monthly.

---

## 4. Gaps nobody serves

1. **Outcome-severity taxonomy.** Cheer Trainer tracks **binary hit/miss**. HitRate's 4-level ladder captures *how* a stunt failed — the data coaches actually argue about. **Uncontested in cheer.**
2. **Athlete-first, not coach/admin-first.** Every cheer "tracking" product today is coach-portal or gym-CRM software. The athlete is a row in someone's roster. The gymnastics world proves athlete-first works (Chalk, ScoreCat); **cheer has no equivalent.**
3. **Shareable cards — for cheer specifically.** Chalk's "Chalkboard" already exports score cards in gymnastics, so the honest gap is **"no cheer app does athlete-first outcome tracking with a shareable card,"** and **nobody anywhere does a holographic / trading-card aesthetic** in-app.
4. **Offline, no-accounts, instant.** Incumbents require login, team setup, or live data feeds. HitRate can own "fast as a tally counter, smart as a cheer app."

**Honest caveats:**
- **Demand is latent, not vocal.** No community clamor found. The market may need to be *created* (TikTok demos, gym ambassadors) rather than *captured*. Flip side: no entrenched competitor, no switching cost.
- **The card is probably the wedge; the dashboard is the retention.** Coaches have the use case but Cheer Trainer got no traction serving them; athletes have the sharing motive.
- **Don't cannibalize the family.** Stay clear of FormationFlow's lane (formations/choreo) and CoachCard/CheerSkillTagger (coach utilities/video).
